## [v1.3] — 2026-06-16

### Changed — LIS2MDL Magnetometer Layout Optimization
- **Removed all copper traces on F.Cu directly below the LIS2MDL** to eliminate trace-induced magnetic field distortion at the sensing element
- **GND copper pour now covers 100% of the area beneath the LIS2MDL** — ensures a complete, symmetric ground reference plane under the sensor and minimizes I2C return-current loop area
- **Repositioned decoupling capacitors** closer to the LIS2MDL VCC pins to shorten the stub length between bypass cap and IC supply pad

### Why these changes matter
A magnetometer is sensitive to magnetic fields generated by nearby current-carrying conductors. Traces routed directly below the sensing die act as fixed magnetic field sources (hard iron offset). A complete GND plane eliminates asymmetric return-current paths, reduces the magnetic loop area of the I2C bus, and provides a stable, calibratable electromagnetic environment.
